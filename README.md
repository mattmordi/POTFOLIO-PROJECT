* Global Economic Analysis Dashboard
1. Project Overview
Analyzed global economic data using SQL Server and Power BI
Explored trends in GDP, population, and economic performance across countries and regions
Built an interactive dashboard to visualize global economic changes over time
2. Objectives
2.1 Global GDP Growth
Global GDP increased from $60T in 2010 to $85T in 2020 (about 42 percent growth)
Growth mainly driven by China, USA, and India
Minor dips observed during global economic slowdowns
Recommended visualization: Line chart of GDP over time
2.2 Population Growth by Region
Global population increased from 6.9B to 7.8B (about 13 percent growth)
Africa: fastest growth (about 2.5 percent per year)
Asia: steady growth (about 1 percent per year)
Europe: almost stagnant (about 0.2 percent per year)
Americas: moderate growth
Oceania: slow growth
Recommended visualization: Stacked area chart by region
2.3 Top GDP Growth Countries (2010 to 2020)
China: +10T GDP growth
USA: +6T GDP growth
India: +3T GDP growth
Germany: +1T GDP growth
Japan: +0.8T GDP growth
Emerging economies such as China and India are key global drivers
Recommended visualization: Bar chart of top countries
3. Key Insights
Global GDP is growing faster than population, improving GDP per capita
Africa has the fastest population growth, showing future economic potential
Large economic gaps still exist between developed and developing regions
Emerging economies are major contributors to global growth
